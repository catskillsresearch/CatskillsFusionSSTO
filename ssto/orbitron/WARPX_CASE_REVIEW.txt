================================================================================
GOAL 1 — WARPX CASE REVIEW: laminar_flow_2d.py (Orbitron crossed-field slice)
================================================================================
Source: ssto/orbitron/laminar_flow_2d.py

1.1 SOLVER AND DIMENSIONALITY
  - PICMI ElectromagneticSolver on a Cartesian2DGrid (WarpX RZ vs XY: here XY).
  - WarpX 2D convention in this script: active plane X–Z, invariant Y; By is
    “out of page” for E×B circulation in X–Z.
  - Time step 1e-12 s, CFL 0.9, max_steps 500 — short diagnostic run, not a
    converged steady-state reactor discharge.

1.2 GEOMETRY (ANALYTIC, NOT MESHED CAD)
  - r_anode = 0.05 m, r_cathode = 0.005 m matches “10 cm tube / 5 mm cathode”
    order of magnitude for a poloidal slice of the device.
  - Boundaries: Dirichlet fields on all sides; particles absorbing — walls are
    ideal sinks, not a real high-voltage feedthrough stack.

1.3 FIELDS
  - E: radial analytic profile derived from V_cathode = -600 kV between
    concentric notional cylinders (log(r) potential). Implemented as Ex, Ez
    with a radial cutoff mask at r >= r_cathode.
  - B: uniform By = 2.0 T (applied field, not self-consistent superconductor).
  - These are “frozen” analytic applied fields — no magnetostatic solve, no
    coil geometry, no ferromagnetics.

1.4 SPECIES AND INITIAL CONDITIONS
  - electrons: annular density 1 cm < r < 3 cm, n_e ~ 5e15 m^-3 (expression),
    initialized with E×B drift momenta consistent with the analytic E field.
    This is a diocotron / hollow-ring style setup, not a fusion burn model.
  - stabilizing_beam: 100 discrete protons on a line at x=0.04 m, inward ux,
    ParticleListDistribution — numerical shear / stabilization artifice.
  - No boron, no alpha ash, no nuclear reaction modules, no neutrals, no
    gas-phase chemistry, no bremsstrahlung deposition back to a solid mesh.

1.5 WHAT THIS CASE IS GOOD FOR
  - Visualizing low-order crossed-field electron dynamics and charge density
    evolution in a cylindrical slice.
  - Pipeline check: pywarpx → diags → yt animation / FG texture path.

1.6 GAPS VS. DESIGN BRIEF (FUSION + ARCJET)
  - Fusion: no p-B11 reactions, no DEC grid as a boundary object, no 4 MV
    terminal physics — only the cathode-side -600 kV scale in E.
  - Arcjet: no compressor inlet BC, no cold air mass flow, no neutral gas
    background, no electron–neutral collisions, no Ohmic heating term tied to
    DEC current channel through the gas.
  - Next extension direction: see laminar_flow_2d_arcjet.py (seed channel,
    optional heavy-ion tracer, expanded diagnostics) and PICMI docs for your
    installed WarpX version for collisions / MCC / field ionization.

1.7 POST-PROCESSING NOTE
  - animate_plasma.py currently expects diags field rho_electrons only.
    laminar_flow_2d_arcjet.py also requests rho_arc_channel_seed; extend the
    yt field list or merge channels before GIF export when running the arcjet deck.
